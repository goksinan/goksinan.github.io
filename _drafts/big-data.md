**Q1. What is big data?**

**A1.** "Big data is a blanket term for any collection of data sets so large or complex that it becomes difficult to process them using traditional data management techniques such as, for example, RDBMS (relational data base management systems)." source: [Cielen et al.](https://www.manning.com/books/introducing-data-science)

The characteristics of big data are:
- *Volume-* How much data is there?
- *Variety-* How diverse are different types of data?
- *Velocity-* At what speed is new data generated?

**Q2. What are the steps of data science process?**

**A2.** It typically consists of 6 steps (source: [Cielen et al.](https://www.manning.com/books/introducing-data-science)):

1. *Setting the research goal-* What is it you want to accomplish?
2. *Collecting data-* Gather the data you will use to answer your research question.
3. *Data preparation-* The raw data will be noisy and untidy. It needs to be prepared for next steps
4. *Data exploration-* To gain a deeper understanding about data. Usually, descriptive statistics, visual techniques, and simple modeling are used.
5. *Model building-* Using the information you gathered and your domain knowledge, you will pick variables and build a statistical model to formulate the research question
6. *Presentation and automation-* Finally, present the results to stakeholders. You may need to automate the system for future use.
